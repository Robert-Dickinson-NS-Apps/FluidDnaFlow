Ideas to Extend or Improve This App

Here are ten directions this could go, ranging from incremental improvements to ambitious pivots:



Tier 1: Quick Enhancements (Days to Weeks)

1. Add More Obstacle Shapes





Airfoils (NACA profiles)



Square/rectangular bluff bodies



Multiple cylinders in tandem or side-by-side



Custom shape drawing tool

Why: Different shapes produce dramatically different flow patterns. Comparing cylinder vs. airfoil teaches drag reduction concepts.



2. Parameter Presets Tied to Real-World Examples





"Flow around a bridge pier" (Re ~10⁵)



"Blood flow in an artery" (Re ~1000-4000)



"Air around a car side mirror" (Re ~10⁴)



"Submarine at cruising speed"

Why: Connects abstract parameters to tangible scenarios. Increases engagement.



3. Export and Sharing





Download simulation as GIF/video



Save parameter sets as shareable URLs



Export velocity/pressure fields as CSV for further analysis

Why: Enables use in presentations, reports, and classroom assignments.



Tier 2: Moderate Enhancements (Weeks to Months)

4. Add Particle Tracing Mode





Release virtual particles that follow streamlines



Visualize residence time, mixing, dispersion



Color particles by velocity or time in domain

Why: Particle tracking is intuitive and visually compelling. Helps explain Lagrangian vs. Eulerian perspectives.



5. Interactive Boundary Conditions





Let users draw walls, add inlets/outlets



Create venturi constrictions, sudden expansions



Model flow around custom obstacles

Why: Increases exploratory potential. Users can test their own hypotheses.



6. Side-by-Side Comparison Mode





Run two simulations simultaneously with different parameters



Sync playback to compare vortex shedding frequency, wake width, drag

Why: Direct comparison accelerates learning. "What happens if I double the Reynolds number?" becomes visually immediate.



7. Educational Overlay Mode





Toggle annotations explaining what's happening physically



Highlight boundary layer separation, stagnation point, recirculation zones



Link to textbook concepts (Bernoulli, Navier-Stokes, Reynolds transport theorem)

Why: Bridges the gap between visualization and formal education. Makes it a genuine teaching tool.



Tier 3: Ambitious Extensions (Months)

8. 3D Visualization Option





Extend to 3D lattice Boltzmann (more computationally intensive)



Use WebGL for rendering



Show 3D streamlines, isosurfaces of vorticity

Why: Real flow is 3D. Even simplified 3D visualization adds significant educational value. Challenging but possible with modern WebGL/WebGPU.



9. Multi-Physics: Heat Transfer





Add temperature field



Simulate convective heat transfer from heated obstacles



Visualize thermal boundary layers

Why: Heat transfer is tightly coupled with fluid dynamics. HVAC, electronics cooling, and many engineering applications need both.



10. AI-Assisted Exploration





Natural language interface: "Show me turbulent flow around a cylinder"



AI explains what's happening: "The alternating vortices you see are called the von Kármán vortex street…"



Suggests experiments: "Try increasing Reynolds number to 200 to see stronger shedding"

Why: This is the MCP Server / Natural Language Query idea applied to CFD education. Connects to our broader Incubation themes.
